*Reed B. Masek,Lindsay Glesener,Jessie Duncan,Kekoa Lasko,Natália Bajnoková,Mary Davenport,Marianne Peterson,Ian Markano,Zasha Avery,Kristopher Cooper,Iain G. Hannah,Brian W. Grefenstette,Stephen M. White,Hugh Hudson,Säm Krucker,David M. Smith,Sarah Paterson*

Main category: astro-ph.SR

TL;DR: 本文利用NuSTAR望远镜对太阳X射线瞬变事件进行了首次统计调查，研究了从活跃区微耀斑到宁静区弱增亮的事件，共分析了113个能量低至10^26 erg的瞬变事件，发现宁静区事件在热学特性上与活跃区事件存在显著差异。

Motivation: X射线与太阳耀斑的初始能量释放和即时加热密切相关，是理解耀斑驱动过程的关键。然而，此前缺乏能够观测活跃区内外微弱X射线事件的高灵敏度望远镜，NuSTAR作为首个长期直接聚焦硬X射线的太阳观测设备，填补了这一空白。

Method: 利用高灵敏度NuSTAR望远镜对太阳X射线瞬变事件进行观测，对113个能量低至10^26 erg的微弱瞬变事件进行统计调查，比较宁静区与活跃区事件的热学和非热学特性，并与RHESSI微耀斑数据进行对比。

Result: 共表征了113个弱能量瞬变事件；与RHESSI微耀斑相比，NuSTAR瞬变事件总体更冷、更暗、光谱稍陡；活跃区瞬变的热能含量与发射等离子体体积无关；宁静区事件平均能量更低、发射体积更小、呈现"冷而亮"而非"热而暗"的特征；未发现宁静区瞬变热能含量超过3×10^27 erg的事件。

Conclusion: NuSTAR能够观测到传统仪器无法探测的微弱太阳X射线事件；活跃区与宁静区瞬变事件的热学特性存在系统性差异，表明可能存在不同的能量释放机制；宁静区热能在3 MK以上等离子体中的释放存在约3×10^27 erg的上限。

Abstract: In this paper, we use a highly sensitive telescope to characterize solar X-ray transients ranging from microflares in active regions down to weakly energetic brightenings in the quiet Sun. X-rays are closely linked to the initial energy release and immediate heating of solar flares, making them invaluable in understanding their driving processes. NuSTAR is the first long-term, direct focusing hard X-ray observatory to have observed the Sun, offering a unique opportunity to search for and characterize X-ray events from inside and outside active regions that would be otherwise unobservable. We present the first statistical survey of NuSTAR solar observations, characterizing the thermal and possibly nonthermal properties of 113 weakly energetic transients down to $10^{26}$ erg, making this the first to directly compare events from the quiet Sun to those in active regions. Relative to RHESSI microflares, our NuSTAR transients are generally cooler, dimmer, and have slightly steeper spectra. Thermal energy content of active region transients appears to be independent of the volume of emitting plasma for transients produced by active regions. This is in contrast to those from the quiet corona, which on average have lower energy content, smaller emission volumes, and appear cool but bright rather than hot but dim, suggesting a break in trends from traditional microflares. We found no quiet Sun transients with a thermal energy content above $3^{27}$ erg, implying an upper limit on the amount of energy released in plasma above 3 MK by quiescent processes.
