Motivation: 首次对星际天体进行中红外光谱表征，研究其化学成分和挥发性物质比例，以了解星际天体的组成特征及其与太阳系彗星的差异。

Method: 使用JWST/MIRI中分辨率光谱仪在2025年12月15-16日和27日对3I/ATLAS进行后近日点观测，获取5-28微米光谱数据，分析荧光特征和挥发性物质产生率。

Result: 检测到水、二氧化碳、甲烷和原子镍等气体成分；甲烷产生相对于水延迟，表明外层物质曾经历耗损；12天内总体排气显著减少；CO₂:H₂O比例远高于典型太阳系彗星，CH₄:H₂O比例也相对富集。

Conclusion: 这是首次在星际天体中直接检测到甲烷，3I/ATLAS的挥发性物质组成与太阳系彗星存在显著差异，表明其形成环境或演化历史不同，为理解星际天体化学组成提供了重要数据。

Abstract: We present the first spectroscopic characterization of an interstellar object at mid-infrared wavelengths. Post-perihelion observations of 3I/ATLAS using the JWST/MIRI medium-resolution spectrometer were obtained on 2025 December 15--16 and 27 when the object was at heliocentric distances of 2.20 and 2.54 au, respectively. Our 5--28 micron spectra exhibit fluorescence features from several gaseous species, including the $ν_2$ band of water at 5.8--7.0 microns. the primary $ν_2$ and associated hot bands of carbon dioxide around 15 microns, and a forbidden transition of atomic nickel at 7.507 microns. We also report the first direct detection of methane in an interstellar object. The delayed onset of methane production relative to water suggests past depletion from the outermost layers, with the observed methane emerging from unprocessed subsurface material. Comparison of the volatile production rates measured during the two epochs indicate a significant reduction in overall outgassing over 12 days, with the measured water activity level dropping more steeply than other species. As shown through near-nucleus coma mapping, 3I continues to display an extended source of water production from icy grains entrained within the coma. Our production rate measurements confirm that 3I exhibits a strongly enhanced CO$_2$:H$_2$O mixing ratio relative to typical solar system comets, as well as a somewhat enriched CH$_4$:H$_2$O value.
